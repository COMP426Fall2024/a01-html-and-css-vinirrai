Lord Hanuman

The Eternal Devotee of Lord Rama

Biography of Lord Hanuman

Lord Hanuman, also known as Anjaneya, is revered as a symbol of strength, devotion, and loyalty. Born to Anjana and Kesari, Hanuman is believed to be an incarnation of Lord Shiva. His undying devotion to Lord Rama is legendary, and he played a crucial role in the Ramayana, especially during the battle against Ravana.

Festivals Celebrating Hanuman

Hanuman Jayanti is the most significant festival dedicated to Lord Hanuman. It is celebrated with great devotion across India, especially in temples where devotees chant Hanuman Chalisa and offer prayers. Special offerings such as sweets and fruits are made to Hanuman, and his statues are adorned with garlands and flowers.

  • Hanuman Jayanti - Celebrated in April
  • Tuesday Offerings - Many devotees offer prayers on Tuesdays
  • Special Pujas during Ram Navami

Hanuman Chalisa

The Hanuman Chalisa is a 40-verse devotional hymn in praise of Lord Hanuman. It was written by the great poet-saint Tulsidas and is recited by millions of devotees for courage, protection, and strength. Here is an excerpt:

Shree Guru Charan Saroj Raj, Nij manu Mukuru Sudhaari
Barnau Raghuvar Bimal Jasu, Jo daayak Phal Chaari
Budhiheen Tanu Jannikay, Sumirow Pavan Kumar
Bal Buddhi Vidya Dehu Mohi, Harahu Kalesh Vikar
